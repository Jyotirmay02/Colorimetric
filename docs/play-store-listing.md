## Full description (max 4000 chars)

```
Spectral Solution Lab is a research utility for chemistry students and analysts who need to extract colour information from images of liquid samples and turn that data into quantitative concentration measurements.

Designed for benchtop colorimetry without specialised equipment, the app lets you photograph a vial, tap to select a region of interest, and instantly read its R, G, B values. Build a calibration curve from samples of known concentration, then predict unknown samples by colour alone.

KEY FEATURES

• 50+ colorimetric equations — including Beer-Lambert log10(I0/I), per-channel absorbance, RGB ratios, channel fractions, luminance, and log-log models — each automatically fitted and ranked by R-squared.

• Calibration workflow — upload images of samples at known concentrations, optionally tag one or more as blanks (I0 reference), and let the app rank the best-fitting equation for your analyte.

• IUPAC-standard Limit of Detection — when two or more blank samples are provided, sigma_blank is computed from replicate blanks and LoD = 3·sigma/slope per IUPAC convention.

• Prediction workflow — capture an unknown sample, extract RGB, and the app predicts concentration using every fitted equation. The best-fit (highest R-squared) prediction is highlighted.

• Region-of-interest selection — auto-center, locked-position, or manual tap. Adjustable ROI size for thin solutions or coloured precipitates.

• Multi-equation overlay graph — visualise up to 6 fits simultaneously with distinct colours and legend, in either linear (concentration vs metric) or log-log space.

• Rich CSV exports — every calibration sample's x and y values for every equation, plus per-equation R-squared, SE, slope, intercept, LoD, and LoQ. Filename auto-tagged with timestamp.

• Cross-device camera locks — flash off, autofocus on, fixed quality, fixed resolution on Android, and a 350 ms settle delay before capture to reduce RGB variance between devices.

• 100% offline and private — all data stays on your device. No account, no sign-in, no cloud upload of your images.

WHO IS THIS FOR

Chemistry undergraduate and postgraduate students, analytical chemistry researchers, classroom demos, low-cost field testing for environmental water samples, and anyone using paper-based or colorimetric kits where a spectrophotometer isn't available.

Developed by Blue Lotus Lab — an academic research project at Maharaja Purna Chandra Autonomous College, Baripada, Odisha, India, by Swagatika Sahu, Biswajit Mohapatra, and Jyotirmay Sethi.
```
